Intel Sustainability Journey

Building a greener future with every milestone

Discover how Intel blends innovation, energy efficiency, and responsible manufacturing to drive sustainable technology across global operations.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

This founding milestone set the course for leadership in energy- efficient computing and long-term sustainability investments.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

The 4004 proved that smaller, smarter chips could deliver better performance with less power.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor established the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

This architecture helped scale Intel's impact on performance, energy use, and global connectivity.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

The 386 helped push performance gains that later reduced energy use per computation.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations, highlighting where sustainability efforts were needed most.

Intel accelerated renewable energy, waste reduction, and cleaner manufacturing after this milestone.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals to advance climate action, water stewardship, and circular manufacturing.

The strategy connects innovation with measurable sustainability goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

This pledge helps drive the company toward low-carbon manufacturing and sustainable design.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, significantly lowering operational carbon emissions.

This result demonstrates how renewable energy is central to Intel's sustainability progress.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders, suppliers, and governments to collaborate on clean manufacturing.

The summit strengthened partnerships and accelerated action on future-ready sustainability solutions.
